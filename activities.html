Bored? Here's what you can do.

Michigan Stadium

Michigan Stadium, nicknamed "The Big House," is the football stadium for the University of Michigan in Ann Arbor, Michigan. It is the largest stadium in the United States and the Western Hemisphere, the third largest stadium in the world, and the 34th largest sports venue in the world.

Hands-On Museum

The Hands-On Museum specializes in interactive exhibits with the goal of helping both children and adults discover the scientist within them by promoting science literacy through experimentation, exploration, and education.

Universtiy of Michigan Museum of Natural History

The Museum of Natural History inspires curiosity with exhibits that explore the natural world from molecules to dinosaurs, a planetarium to discover the Universe, science demonstrations, and U-M scientists discussing their research.

Matthaei Botanical Garden

The Matthaei Botanical Gardens is an outdoor garden space featuring bonsai, native plants, and perennials, a garden created just for kids, nearly 3 miles of trails, and an indoor conservatory filled with plants from around the world.

Michigan Theater

The Michigan Theater shows independent films and stage productions, and hosts musical concerts.

Hill Auditorium

The Hill Auditorium is the largest performance venue on the University of Michigan campus.

Hudson Mills Metropark

The Hudson Mills Metropark features some of the most picturesque spots on the Huron River and is popular with anglers, hikers and paddlers alike. This park features some of the most picturesque spots on the Huron and is popular with anglers, hikers and paddlers alike.

Pinball Pete's

Pinball Pete's has been a top source of fun and entertainment for many decades with pinball machines & video games, plus pool tables, skee-ball, air hockey & more.

University of Michigan Museum of Art

The University of Michigan Museum of Art is one of the finest and largest university art museums in the country holding collections representing 150 years of art collecting.
